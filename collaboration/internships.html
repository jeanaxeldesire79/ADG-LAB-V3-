Internships & Residencies

Build a residency that blends learning and impact. Axel Dev Lab welcomes students and emerging professionals eager to experiment in public-interest tech, policy design, and creative storytelling.

Applications open

Design your learning residency at Axel Dev Lab.

Our internship cycles pair emerging talent with seasoned mentors to build products, research, and narratives that move public systems forward. Each residency is scoped to stretch your craft while producing measurable impact.

Program length

10–16 weeks

Cohorts in Spring • Summer • Fall

Tracks

Data · Policy · Creative

What we expect

Bridge curiosity, craft, and community impact.

Interns join live projects that span development analytics, policy design, and creative storytelling. We look for residents who bring initiative, humility, and a hunger to learn in multidisciplinary teams.

Curiosity & initiative

Ask thoughtful questions, test ideas quickly, and move from ambiguity to clarity with self-direction.

Applied research mindset

Translate academic rigor into products—dashboards, briefs, creative outputs—that stakeholders can act upon.

Collaborative builder

Partner with designers, engineers, strategists, and community voices—respecting feedback loops and shared ownership.

How to become one

A three-step journey designed for exploration and fit.

Every application cycle prioritises thoughtful storytelling over speed. Use each step to show us how you think, what excites you, and how you want to grow.

Step 01

Explore your track

Tell us your focus area, campus affiliation, and the problem space you want to unlock. We review submissions on a rolling basis and shortlist profiles weekly.

Step 02

Challenge sprint

Complete a short brief—data analysis, concept note, or storytelling storyboard—paired with reflective prompts about learnings and trade-offs.

Step 03

Mentor alignment

Meet your prospective mentor and squad. Together we co-create a learning plan, project milestones, and success markers before extending an offer.

What you gain

Experience that accelerates your mission-driven career.

Project ownership

Lead a core workstream with tangible deliverables—whether that is a dataset, prototype, policy memo, or creative narrative for a live client.

Mentorship & coaching

Weekly sprints with staff mentors, office hours with practice leads, and access to our learning library and masterclasses.

Career visibility

Published credits on outputs, recommendation letters, and an alumni community that shares openings and fellowships.

Materials to submit

Curate a thoughtful submission packet.

Show us your curiosity, your craft, and the themes that energise you. Feel free to include multimedia links—just provide brief context for each piece.

  • • Résumé (PDF) highlighting quantitative/qualitative coursework, tools, and language fluency
  • • One-page personal statement describing your learning goals and impact thesis
  • • Sample work (analysis, visualization, research memo, or creative piece) with a short context note
  • • Availability window, timezone, and preferred modality (remote, hybrid, in-person)
